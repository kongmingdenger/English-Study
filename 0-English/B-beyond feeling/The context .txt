Anyone who wishes to master an activity must first understand its tools and rules. This is as true of critical thinking as it is of golf, carpentry, flying a plane, or brain surgery. 
“任何一个想精通（master 完全控制）某项活动的人，首先必须知道它的工具和规则。这就跟批判性思考一样（as……as…… 跟什么什么一样），比如打高尔夫球、做木工（carpentry)、驾驶飞机或做脑外科手术(surgery)。

In critical thinking, however, the tools are not material objects but concepts, and the rules govern mental rather than physical performance.
然而（however），在批判性思考中，工具不是物质的（material）客体（objects，对象，客体），而是概念，其规则是支配（govern）认知（mental 智力的，内心、精神）而不是身体的（physical 身体的、物质的）运作（performance 上演，表现 ）。


This first section explores seven important concepts，individuality, critical thinking, truth, knowledge, opinion, evidence, and argument with a chapter devoted to each. 
这第一篇主要探讨（explores）七个重要概念（concepts），个性（individuality）、批判性思考、真理、知识、观点、证据，以及每章讨论一个概念。



Most of these concepts are so familiar that you may be inclined to wonder whether there is any point to examining them. The answer is yes, for three reasons.
这些概念大多为人所熟知，以至你也许会疑惑（inclined 弯下腰的）：考察这些概念是否有意义？答案是肯定的，其理由有三：


First, much of what is commonly believed about these concepts is mistaken. Second, who ever examines them carefully is always rewarded with fresh insights. Third, the more thorough your knowledge of these concepts, the more proficient you will be in your thinking.
第一，对这些概念的普遍看法（commonly believed）是误解的；第二，无论谁仔细考察这些概念，总会赢得（rewarded）新的见识（fresh insights）；第三，你越是透彻地了解这些概念，就越能精通于（proficient 熟练，精通）思维。”
